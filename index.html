    Short Bio

    Currently, I am an Academy of Finland postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science at Aalto University, Finland. Together with Arno Solin, Trevor Campbell, Guy van den Broeck, and Zoubin Ghahramani, I am developing tools for efficient computations in stochastic process models and Bayesian deep learning by utilizing core ideas of probabilistic circuits. Previously, I was a postdoc with Arno Solin (2020 – 2022). Before that, I finished my PhD thesis on “Sum-Product Networks for Complex Modelling Tasks” at Graz University of Technology, Austria, under the supervision of Franz Pernkopf and Robert Peharz in 2020. For more details see my academic cv.

    Research Interests

    I am broadly interested in principled approaches to probabilistic reasoning, uncertainty quantification, deep architectures, and nonparametric modelling families. Currently, I am particularly interested in pushing the frontier of tractability and flexibility by exploring techniques based on tractable models (e.g., probabilistic circuits), deep learning and Bayesian nonparametrics.

    In addition, I am part of the Turing.jl project and co-organise the ELLIS Seminar on Advances in Probabilistic Machine Learning.
